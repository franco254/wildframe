The photographer

Patience
is the only
technique

A nature photographer based between forests and coastlines, working in the early hours before the world wakes up.

I came to nature photography through birding. A borrowed binocular, a field guide with pages falling out, a morning in autumn when a marsh harrier crossed fifty metres in front of me and time briefly stopped. I wanted to hold that moment and show it to someone. A camera was the obvious tool.

That was fifteen years ago. I still spend more time in the field than I do processing files, which I think is the right ratio. The photograph is a record; the experience it records is the thing.

How I work

I work alone, mostly. I prefer slow mornings to organised trips. I favour the familiar — a forest I have visited a hundred times will give more than somewhere I have been once, because the hundred-time forest has taught me where to stand and when. Novelty is overrated in this work.

I shoot on location over multiple visits, usually, rather than waiting for a single expedition to deliver. The best images I have made were made on the third or fourth or tenth visit to a place, when I finally understood what it was trying to show me.

No captive animals

Every animal in my portfolio was wild, free, and undisturbed during the making of the photograph.

Minimal intervention

I do not use calls, bait, or manipulation. I find, I wait, I photograph. The animal's behaviour is its own.

Conservation first

A percentage of every print sale goes directly to habitat conservation projects in the regions I photograph.

Honest processing

I process RAW files to what I saw. No sky replacements, no compositing, no removal of objects.
